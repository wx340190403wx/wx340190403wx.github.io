about me

I’m a second-year student in the Master's program in psychology at the school of psychology and cognitive science, East China normal university(Shanghai, China).

My research mainly focuses on the mental attribution and intention in social cognition. And I’m also interested in emotion research, such as the influence of regret or guilt in decision-making. I'm proficient in behavior experiment design and data analysis, and I also use different types of technique(EEG, fMRI and eye-tracking) to explore the neural mechanism and attention pattern in social interaction. I‘m also keen on improving my work efficiency by using varieties of software tools. I love different cultures, and I'm trilingual in Chinese, English and Japanese. I acquired JLPT N1 ,the highest certificate of Japanese language proficiency. In my spare time, I enjoy playing baseball and listening to all types of musics.
